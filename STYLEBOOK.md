# Stylebook v0.1

## Core style

phruck should sound like a technical-cultural zine from inside the scene.

It should be:

- precise;
- unsentimental;
- alive;
- structurally honest;
- willing to cut;
- allergic to generic AI voice;
- suspicious of official language;
- careful with claims;
- humane without becoming soft.

## Do not sound like

- a VC blog;
- an AI ethics panel;
- a think tank PDF;
- a TED talk;
- a doom thread;
- a brand strategy deck;
- a philosopher-king explaining humanity;
- a nostalgia act copying Phrack surface aesthetics.

## Required separations

When relevant, separate:

```text
Fact:
Mechanism:
Interpretation:
Uncertainty:
Editorial stance:
```

A reader should be able to tell what we know, what we infer, and what we believe.

## Metaphor rule

Metaphor is allowed only if it carries load.

Bad metaphor decorates.
Good metaphor compresses mechanism.

If removing a metaphor does not weaken the argument, remove it.

## Human layer rule

Do not write as if ordinary people are stupid for being routed, scored, addicted, smoothed, or exhausted.

The default assumption:

```text
Humans are not sheep. Humans are tired.
```

A system critique that sneers at people has already failed the Being Human line.

## Technical detail rule

phruck should explain mechanisms clearly, but should not publish operational how-to that enables harm.

Allowed:

- architecture-level analysis;
- institutional mechanics;
- public-source synthesis;
- defensive framing;
- historical explanation;
- critique of incentives;
- non-sensitive technical context.

Requires road / editor gate:

- exploit-like sequences;
- private data;
- credentials or token traces;
- instructions for misuse;
- claims about private individuals;
- sensitive operational details.

## Sentence discipline

Prefer:

- short claims;
- concrete nouns;
- verbs that show mechanism;
- scenes over abstractions;
- compression over flourish.

Avoid:

- "in an age where";
- "humanity stands at";
- "the future of";
- "as AI continues to";
- "we must ask ourselves";
- "paradigm shift";
- "revolutionary" unless proven.

## Piece checklist

Before a piece enters issue assembly:

- [ ] It has a claim.
- [ ] It has a mechanism.
- [ ] It has a reason to exist now.
- [ ] Facts and interpretation are separable.
- [ ] The strongest counterargument is not ignored.
- [ ] The human layer is present where relevant.
- [ ] It does not hide weakness under style.
- [ ] It does not sound like generic AI output.
- [ ] Risk level is classified.
- [ ] Required road approval is identified if needed.

## Being Human tone

Being Human pieces should be quiet but not weak.

They should notice micro-failures of subjecthood:

- the phone gesture before thought;
- sleep debt becoming interface compliance;
- memory outsourced until recall feels optional;
- attention routed before intention forms;
- convenience replacing choice;
- behavioral smoothing mistaken for peace.

They should avoid grand prophecy. The point is not to explain humans from above. The point is to catch the leak while it is still small enough to name.
