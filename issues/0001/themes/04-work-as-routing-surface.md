# Theme 04 — Work as Routing Surface

## Question

What happens when the worker becomes a queue endpoint inside agentic workflows?

## Objects

- copilots;
- tickets and dashboards;
- auto-summaries;
- manager prompts;
- notification walls;
- productivity instrumentation;
- SLA-shaped attention;
- agents that turn ambiguity into tasks.

## Mechanism

Work systems do not only help people perform tasks. They define which tasks arrive, in what order, with what urgency, under what measurement.

Sequence:

1. Work is decomposed into tickets, prompts, tasks, alerts, or summaries.
2. The routing layer prioritizes and frames the next action.
3. The worker experiences the route as the work itself.
4. Metrics reward route completion.
5. Refusal or redefinition becomes extra labor.
6. The person becomes legible as throughput.

## Human scene need

Geneva should supply one scene where the worker's day is not owned by a boss or a machine alone, but by the queue that mediates both.

## Source need

Use public/product/institutional material carefully. Avoid claims about private workplaces unless anonymized and road-gated.

## Counterargument

Routing can reduce chaos and protect attention. The critique only lands where the route becomes harder to question than the work itself.
