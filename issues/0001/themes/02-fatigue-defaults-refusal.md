# Theme 02 — Fatigue, Defaults, Refusal

## Question

Why do tired people become easier to route without being stupid, passive, or sheep?

## Spine line

```text
Humans are not sheep. Humans are tired.
```

## Mechanism

Refusal has a cost. Defaults lower the cost of compliance. Fatigue changes the exchange rate.

Sequence:

1. The system presents a default.
2. Refusal requires attention, time, or social cost.
3. Fatigue makes the refusal cost feel irrational.
4. The default is accepted as relief.
5. Repetition makes the accepted default feel like preference.
6. Preference becomes profile, score, route, or policy.

## Forms this can become

- Harmedi memo: the fatigue surface.
- One mechanism note: default law.
- Zero opener section: why refusal got expensive.

## Source need

Before publication, source or clearly label claims around:

## Tone risk

This lane fails if it mocks the person for taking the default. The target is the pricing of refusal, not the exhausted user.

## Counterargument

Defaults can protect attention. Automation can reduce waste. The critique must distinguish useful delegation from silent removal of meaningful refusal.
