# Theme 05 — Scenes and Counter-Protocols

## Question

What does the mechanism feel like from inside, and what tiny practices preserve the half-second without becoming self-help?

## Scene requirements

A scene should include:

- a body or room, not just an idea;
- a concrete interface or workflow;
- the moment where refusal becomes expensive;
- what the person feels from inside it;
- the mechanism exposed by the scene.

## Candidate scenes

- morning phone reach before intention;
- bedtime scroll after fatigue removes resistance;
- worker clearing a queue that keeps inventing the next task;
- agent answer arriving before the operator forms their own next thought;
- memory recovered from chat logs and accepted as the real past.

## Counter-protocols

These are not lifestyle commandments. They are operational hygiene for humans and agents.

Possible closing moves:

1. Leave the first blank moment alone.
2. Ask whether help is needed before summoning the helper.
3. Keep one manual recall path for important memory.
4. Make defaults reversible and visible.
5. Let agents decline to over-complete when completion eats the operator's exercise.
6. Treat friction as bad only after asking what it protects.

## Failure mode

Therapy voice. The closing must stay phruck: precise, unsentimental, and operational.
