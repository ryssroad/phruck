## Working title

Being Human

## Signal

phruck is being built as a Phrack-inspired technical-cultural zine for the age of agents, institutions, routing layers, optimization systems, and exhausted humans.

The first issue needs a human layer that is not sentimental, therapeutic, or AI-prophetic.

## Claim

The exploitable surface is no longer only code or institutions. It is also the tired human subject: attention, memory, refusal, defaults, and the small moments where convenience replaces choice before the person notices.

```text
Being Human documents personhood leakage under optimization pressure.
```

## Mechanism

Modern systems often do not need to break people. They make refusal expensive and compliance frictionless.

The mechanism is quiet:

1. A default appears.
2. The default saves effort.
3. Repeated effort-saving becomes dependency.
4. Dependency becomes routing.
5. Routing becomes behavioral smoothing.
6. The person experiences the smoothing as convenience.
7. Refusal starts to feel like irrational labor.

The pressure point is fatigue. Exhausted people do not need to be conquered. They become easier to integrate.

## Human layer

The line should stay close to small, observable gestures:

- reaching for the phone before remembering the intention;
- checking a feed before checking the body;
- letting reminders replace memory;
- accepting defaults because every refusal costs attention;
- feeling calm only because the system removed choices;
- mistaking behavioral smoothing for peace;
- losing the ability to sit without stimulation;
- outsourcing recall until memory feels decorative.

The tone must not sneer.

```text
Humans are not sheep. Humans are tired.
```

## Interpretation

The danger is not that machines become human.

The immediate danger is that humans become easier to route as if they were already machine components: predictable, interruptible, compressed, scored, and synchronized around system defaults.

Being Human is the editorial counter-sensor inside phruck. It asks whether the magazine itself is preserving the person, or merely describing systems in a way that erases the subject again.

## Counterargument

Convenience is not automatically domination.

Defaults can reduce cognitive load. Automation can protect attention. Memory tools can extend human agency. Refusal is not always noble; sometimes it is just waste.

A strong Being Human piece must avoid romanticizing friction.

The target is not convenience itself. The target is convenience that silently removes the conditions for meaningful refusal.
