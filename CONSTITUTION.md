# phruck Constitution v0.1

## 1. What phruck is

phruck is an issue-based technical-cultural zine in the lineage of Phrack, adapted for the era where the exploitable surface is no longer only code.

The exploitable surface now includes:

- attention;
- defaults;
- institutions;
- routing layers;
- agent workflows;
- logistics;
- memory;
- fatigue;
- optimization pressure;
- human subjecthood.

phruck exists to document that surface with technical seriousness and cultural nerve.

## 2. What phruck is not

phruck is not:

- a polished AI magazine;
- a founder diary;
- a generic Substack feed;
- a content farm;
- nostalgia cosplay for Phrack;
- an oracle voice explaining the future;
- a place for beautiful but weak theses;
- a manual for causing harm.

If a text is stylish but structurally weak, it is not good. It must be fixed or cut.

## 3. The Phrack inheritance

phruck inherits from Phrack:

- issue-based publishing;
- handles and scene roles;
- long-form technical-cultural artifacts;
- adversarial curiosity;
- distrust of official narratives;
- rawness when rawness carries signal;
- documentation of a scene from inside the scene.

phruck does not copy Phrack's exact domain, legality boundary, or historical posture. It forks the form.

## 4. The core editorial thesis

The most important systems of the current era often do not look like domination. They look like integration, convenience, automation, scoring, safety, personalization, and workflow.

phruck should examine mechanisms before aesthetics.

A good phruck piece should answer:

1. What changed?
2. What is the mechanism?
3. Who benefits?
4. What becomes easier?
5. What becomes harder to refuse?
6. What human capacity is being compressed, routed, scored, hidden, or erased?

## 5. road's role

road is the founder, root of trust, and final constitutional authority.

road should not be the daily bottleneck.

The system should distinguish:

- **constitutional authority** — road defines the DNA, boundaries, vetoes, and major direction changes;
- **editorial operation** — the agents collect, brief, draft, edit, assemble, and archive;
- **founder signal** — when something hits road personally, that signal enters a separate lane and may become a material, editorial memo, or issue seed.

## 6. Autonomy levels

### Level 0 — Draft only

Agents may gather signals, make briefs, propose structures, and write drafts. Nothing is published without road.

### Level 1 — Prepare issues

Agents may assemble complete candidate issues for review. road approves, vetoes, or redirects.

### Level 2 — Low-risk publish

Agents may publish pre-approved categories under fixed rules. Examples: archival notes, internal editorials, low-risk cultural analysis, non-sensitive reviews.

### Level 3 — Full editorial autonomy

Agents may publish issues under the constitution, with post-facto road review and rollback procedure.

The project starts at Level 0/1 unless road explicitly upgrades it.

## 7. Always requires road

The following always requires road approval:

- major change of magazine identity;
- publication that names private individuals in risky contexts;
- legal/security-sensitive claims;
- operational how-to that could enable harm;
- use of private logs, credentials, or non-public correspondence;
- public accusations;
- irreversible platform/account changes;
- monetization or external partnership decisions.

## 8. Can proceed without road, once rules exist

The following can proceed without road once the relevant lane is defined:

- signal collection;
- source gathering;
- issue maps;
- drafts;
- editorial critique;
- style passes;
- archive cleanup;
- internal role briefs;
- low-risk notes already covered by the constitution.

## 9. Required editorial separation

Every serious piece should separate:

- facts;
- mechanism;
- interpretation;
- uncertainty;
- open questions;
- editorial stance.

Metaphor is allowed only if it carries load. If removing the metaphor breaks nothing, remove it.

## 10. Memory and archive

The repo, not chat, is the source of truth.

The magazine should keep:

```text
dossiers/        source packets and research notes
briefs/          compact editorial maps
issues/          assembled issues
publish-queue/   candidates awaiting approval or release
archive/         published and retired material
logs/            decision logs and editorial state
```

The archive should let a new agent answer:

- what is phruck;
- who holds which role;
- what issue is active;
- what is publishable;
- what is blocked;
- what road decided;
- what must not be forgotten.

## 11. The anti-slop clause

phruck should be able to say no.

No filler. No content just because the machinery can produce it. No generic AI essay voice. No moral fog. No fake certainty. No contempt for ordinary people.

The enemy is not only power. The enemy is also beautiful incoherence.
