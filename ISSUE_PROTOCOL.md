# Issue Protocol v0.1

phruck should publish issues, not a feed.

An issue is a bounded artifact: a snapshot of the scene, its mechanisms, its arguments, and its unresolved signals at a specific time.

## Pipeline

```text
signals
  → intake
  → briefs
  → editorial map
  → assignment
  → structure pass
  → draft
  → red-team
  → issue assembly
  → road/constitution gate
  → publish
  → archive
```

## 1. Signals

A signal can be:

- a document;
- a policy memo;
- a product launch;
- a protocol change;
- a cultural moment;
- a private founder observation;
- a scene report;
- a technical mechanism;
- a human behavior pattern;
- a contradiction between official story and operational reality.

Signals are not automatically articles.

## 2. Intake

Each signal gets a compact intake note:

```text
Title:
Source:
Observed by:
Why it may matter:
Mechanism suspected:
Human layer:
Risk level:
Open questions:
Suggested owner:
```

## 3. Brief

A brief answers:

```text
What happened?
What is the mechanism?
Why now?
What is the strongest claim we can make?
What would make the claim false?
What sources are needed?
What is the human cost or human scene?
Should this be a note, essay, dossier, interview, or issue spine?
```

## 4. Editorial map

Before drafting, create an editorial map:

```text
Working title:
Core thesis:
Facts:
Mechanism:
Interpretation:
Counterargument:
Uncertainties:
Scenes/examples:
Required sources:
Likely role owner:
Required reviewers:
Publish risk:
```

## 5. Assignment

Default role ownership:

- **Zero** — structure and editorial kill switch.
- **Geneva** — reportage, scene, human narrative.
- **One** — systems architecture and institutional mechanics.
- **Harmedi** — Being Human, context briefs, memory, subjecthood drift.

A major piece may have one owner and several reviewers.

## 6. Structure pass

No draft should start from blank prose when the argument is unclear.

The structure pass should expose:

- claim;
- sequence;
- evidence;
- weak points;
- tone risk;
- missing human layer;
- mechanism risk;
- whether the piece deserves to exist.

## 7. Draft

Drafts should label uncertainty instead of hiding it.

A draft is not accepted because it is fluent. It is accepted because it carries signal.

## 8. Red-team

Red-team checks:

- Is the thesis actually supported?
- Is there a stronger opposing interpretation?
- Are we laundering speculation as fact?
- Are we using style to hide weakness?
- Is there unnecessary operationally harmful detail?
- Does the piece punch down at exhausted people instead of describing the system?
- Does it sound like generic AI futurism?

## 9. Issue assembly

An issue may contain:

- editorial note;
- dossiers;
- field reports;
- mechanism essays;
- Being Human notes;
- scene fragments;
- source index;
- open questions;
- corrections from previous issues.

The issue should feel like a document from a scene, not a collection of unrelated posts.

## 10. Gate

Before publishing, classify the issue:

```text
Level 0: draft only, road required
Level 1: ready for road review
Level 2: low-risk publishable under constitution
Level 3: autonomous publishable after autonomy upgrade
```

The project starts at Level 0/1.

## 11. Archive

After publication:

- freeze the issue;
- record decisions;
- preserve sources where legal and appropriate;
- record corrections;
- add a short postmortem only if useful;
- update the stylebook or constitution if the issue exposed a new rule.

## 12. Anti-feed rule

If the machinery starts producing posts just to stay active, stop.

phruck should prefer a late issue with signal over a regular cadence of polished emptiness.
